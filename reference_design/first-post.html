Starting a blog

2025.07.30

I have been wanting to start a blog for a while. Not because the world needs another blog, but because I need a place to put things — thoughts, notes, fragments. A workshop for writing, not a stage for performance.

The web has become a strange place. Every platform wants you to post, to engage, to optimise. But a personal site can be something else entirely. A corner of the internet that answers to no algorithm and carries no metric but your own satisfaction.

I built this site with Astro and a theme called Chiri. It is minimal by design — no JavaScript framework on the page, no tracking, no social widgets. Just HTML, a little CSS, and a typeface I like.

A personal site is a workshop for writing, not a stage for performance.

The hardest part was not the building. It was the deciding — deciding that what I have to say is worth publishing, even if it is imperfect, even if nobody reads it. I am still not sure I believe that. But here we are.

I plan to write about:

  • Software and the craft of building things
  • Reading and what I am learning
  • The occasional personal note

If you want to reach me, I am on Bluesky or you can email me at the address in the footer. Thanks for reading.

This is the first of what I hope will be many posts. If you are reading this, thank you for being here.

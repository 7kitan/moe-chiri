Album Art

Some favourite album covers — each a masterclass in visual communication.

OK Computer — Radiohead, 1997

The album that taught me how a record can feel like a cohesive world rather than a collection of songs.

Kind of Blue — Miles Davis, 1959

Unknown Pleasures — Joy Division, 1979

Peter Saville's design is proof that the strongest visuals are often the simplest.

Discovery — Daft Punk, 2001

Loveless — My Bloody Valentine, 1991

The cover matches the music perfectly — blurred, visceral, impossible to pin down.

In Rainbows — Radiohead, 2007

Geogaddi — Boards of Canada, 2002

Nostalgia as a design language. The warm, worn aesthetic tells you what the music sounds like before you press play.

Doolittle — Pixies, 1989
